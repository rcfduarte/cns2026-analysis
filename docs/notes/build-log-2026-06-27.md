### Key fix during build
`esearch "BMC Neurosci"[Journal] AND <year>[PDAT]` returns the **whole journal**, not
just the CNS supplement — regular research articles (DOI `…/s12868-…`) were polluting
the corpus and collapsing to a `P0` fallback id (100 false dupes in 2015). Fix: keep a
record only if its DOI matches the supplement pattern `-S<n>-<CODE>` (`is_supplement_abstract`),
and broaden code prefixes beyond `[OPA]` to capture featured/keynote (`F`, `K`, `I`, `T`, `W`).
After the fix: 0 dupes in 2015, clean type split.

### ⚠ 2008 / 2009 PMC gap (data availability, not a bug)
CNS*2008 (vol 9 S1) and CNS*2009 (vol 10 S1) individual abstracts are **not deposited in
PMC** as separate articles: `10.1186/1471-2202-{9,10}-S1-P1[DOI]` resolves to nothing, and
`<year>[PDAT]` returns only other BMC supplements (S2/S3) plus regular articles. These two
years must be recovered from the **BMC supplement landing pages** (HTML) if needed:
- 2008: https://bmcneurosci.biomedcentral.com/articles/supplements/volume-9-supplement-1
- 2009: https://bmcneurosci.biomedcentral.com/articles/supplements/volume-10-supplement-1

Deferred — a small HTML-scrape fetcher would close the gap; out of scope for the PMC-based
foundation.

Era B is **`<sec>`-structured** (one top-level `<sec>` per abstract) but stores the byline and
affiliations as **nested `<sec>` titles** (no `<contrib>`/`<aff>` tags), with affiliations glued
like `1Inst A, USA2Inst B`. Unified `_assemble()` parses author/affiliation/body for both Era B
(nested-sec) and Era C (flat-`<p>`), tolerant of spaced and glued superscript markers.

## 2008 / 2009 gap — CLOSED via BMC HTML ✅

Individual abstracts are not in PMC but exist as open-access **BMC HTML articles**
(`bmc_html.py`): scrape the paginated supplement TOC (50/page) for DOIs, fetch each article,
take title+authors+affiliations from JSON-LD and the body from `c-article-body`. Needs a
browser User-Agent (BMC 403s bots). Result: **2008 = 179, 2009 = 399** abstracts.
